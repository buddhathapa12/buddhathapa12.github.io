Welcome to the first post in a new series focused on bridging bioinformatics engineering with clean frontend architecture.

By moving the portfolio into Next.js, I created a simpler authoring workflow for content and made the site more discoverable with route-based metadata and static generation.

## What changed

- Reorganized the portfolio into App Router routes
- Added a blog index and dynamic post pages
- Kept the site fast with static HTML and minimal dependencies

## Why it matters

Bioinformatics teams deserve documentation and technical writing that is easy to maintain. A well-structured portfolio and blog help research engineers share their work with clarity.
